About Climate

Climate refers to the long-term patterns of temperature, humidity, wind, precipitation, and other atmospheric conditions in a given area. Unlike weather, which can change daily, climate represents the average conditions in a region over extended periods (typically 30 years or more).

Key Elements of Climate:

Temperature: The average temperatures, including seasonal highs and lows.


Precipitation: The type (rain, snow, sleet), frequency, and amount of precipitation a region receives.


Humidity: The amount of moisture in the air, which can affect temperatures and precipitation.


Wind: Average wind patterns, which can influence temperatures and precipitation.


Seasonal Variability: How temperatures, precipitation, and other factors change across seasons.


Clouds : Play a major role in the climate system by scattering sunlight and absorbing heat radiation from the earth


Ocean Currents: Play a vital role in climate control by moving heat towards the equator.


Solar radiation : The fundamental energy driving the climate system and nearly all climate and biologic processes on Earth are dependent on solar input.
